This is a remarkable time to be involved with VPM.

The organization celebrated its 60th anniversary by delivering award-winning content and experiences to communities across Virginia, all during a year of unprecedented challenges for public media.

In 2026, VPM will begin its next chapter by opening the doors to our new home in the heart of downtown Richmond. Our headquarters will feature a modern newsroom with over 25 staff, state-of-the art media production studios, and community spaces that will enable people to convene, converse and learn.

With our new location and exceptional facilities, VPM is positioned to engage more Virginians and deepen our impact for the next 60 years. We can't wait to welcome you!

2025 Highlights

Making an Impact in Our Communities

Across Central Virginia and the Shenandoah Valley, VPM was present and engaged in your community throughout 2025.

May 2025

Chasing Cambodia Premiere

More than 400 people joined VPM at Richmond's Byrd Theater in May to be a part of the premiere of Chasing Cambodia, a documentary that explores one cyclist's journey to finding the balance between his Cambodian heritage and American upbringing. Following the screening, the directors, producers and film subject Johnny Phan took part in a panel conversation about the importance of honoring and celebrating Asian American heritage.

February 2025

Education Forum

VPM sparked engaging conversations in February with our Education Forum. Moderated by the Honorable Anne B. Holton, Professor of Education Policy at George Mason University, the forum brought together more than 100 people to discuss opportunity gaps facing Virginia students; teacher recruitment and retention challenges; and innovative solutions by local school districts to address these issues.

April 2025

Take Your Child to Work Day

It's Take Your Child to Work Day! In April, VPM and Clark Construction Group welcomed kids of construction trade and craft workers to our future headquarters site for a day of learning and fun. Even in our construction phase, we're always looking to share knowledge with the next generation.

September 2025

Life in the Heart Land

On September 18, more than 150 people celebrated season 3 of Life in the Heart Land at the Sipe Center in Bridgewater in Rockingham County. With panel discussions, screenings, artisan demonstrations and musical performances, the event helped community members understand the work being done in rural communities to build meaningful, positive outcomes.

October 2025

Richmond Folk Festival

Style Weekly and VPM Music celebrated at the 21st Richmond Folk Festival in October. VPM Music hosted a live broadcast of the festival so listeners could tune in all weekend long from their homes, cars or phones. Style Weekly set up a Listening Lounge for festival goers to kick back and relax or take part in an on-site screen-printing activity.

September 2025

Healthy Birth Healthy Baby Expo

In September, VPM joined more than 30 community partners for the 2025 Healthy Birth Healthy Baby Expo in Charlottesville, an annual free event that celebrates community, pregnancy, birth and babies. During the expo, Terri Allard, VPM's Education & Engagement Manager for Charlottesville, and Hannah Davis-Reid, VPM News reporter, shared vital educational resources to help families engage with their children in meaningful ways.

Milestone

Virginia Home Grown - 25 Years

Happy anniversary to VPM's longest running series, Virginia Home Grown! In 2025, host Peggy Singlemann and team kicked off the 25th season of the fan-favorite show that highlights the unique richness of gardens and natural ecosystems throughout Virginia.

Journalism

News

In 2025, the media landscape continued to change, and VPM met the challenges with strategy and innovation. We provided Virginians with local news and election coverage, added ways to consume news content and expanded coverage of the Shenandoah Valley. In 2025, the VPM newsroom remained one of the largest newsrooms in the state.

Capitol Correspondents Team

If you're walking through the Virginia State Capitol during session, chances are you will run into the VPM News capitol correspondents team, made up of state politics reporter Jahd Khalil and multimedia journalist Shaban Athuman. This assignment isn't something they take lightly.

State Coverage

"As journalists, we have a responsibility to uncover and spotlight what our state government is doing," says Jahd. What are the secrets to this duo's success? "Building relationships, so we can have access to the senators, delegates and staff to ask questions," Jahd explains. "And knowing that we're capturing history is a huge motivator," adds Shaban.

Jahd Khalil & Shaban Athuman

VPM News Capitol Correspondents Team

Podcasts

Staying Plugged in with Podcasts

2025 was a big year for VPM's podcast RVA's Got Issues as it launched both its second and third seasons covering the politics and big issues defining life in and around Richmond. New to the podcast line-up in 2025 was The Virginia Press Room, featuring reporters from the Virginia Press Corps offering a behind-the-scenes look at state politics and policies.

Election Coverage

Covering All the Races

In November's election, all three statewide offices and all 100 members of the House of Delegates were on the ballot, and the VPM News team prepared for months for this consequential Virginia election. "We're sharing our news stories across multiple platforms and in multiple formats – from YouTube to radio and from quick updates to in-depth analyses," explained Elliott Robinson, VPM's news director.

Learning Resources

Education

Throughout 2025, VPM continued to be an educational partner for families, caregivers and educators of the Commonwealth's children. Through community events, partnerships and programming, we provide families with learning resources to help kids in their earliest years.

Community Partnership

Sharing Resources in Impactful Places

In July, VPM delivered posters, Sesame Street materials, booklets, videos and other early learning resources to five Charlottesville-area WIC (Women, Infants & Children) offices. "Our close partnerships with community organizations like the Anne & Gene Worrell Foundation and the Blue Ridge Health District make this impactful work possible," explained Mary Jo Grieve, VPM's director of early childhood care and education.

WIC Offices Anne & Gene Worrell Foundation
Campaign

Spreading the Message

Did you know that 90% of a child's brain develops by the age of 5? That is why it's important for children to have early, positive interactions that make a lasting difference. To spread this message, VPM partnered with the Robins Foundation and Thrive Birth to Five to develop a broadcast and digital campaign called "90by5." As a result of the campaign, more than 5,000 people visited 90by5.org to learn more about the importance of a child's earliest days of learning.

Digital Series

Exploring Solutions in Science

Zero Waste, a YouTube series focused on sustainability, offers solutions to help people improve their health, finances and the environment. In 2025, more than 40,000 people watched episodes on how to grow your own plants, manage sustainable lawn care and fix fast fashion, while ultimately helping to slow climate change.

News Expansion

Growing the Newsroom

VPM expanded our news coverage in Central Virginia and the Shenandoah Valley. In May, we welcomed Hannah Davis-Reid to the VPM News team as our new general assignment reporter based in Charlottesville.

From Classroom to Newsroom

VPM welcomes students to our headquarters to learn about media and community engagement … and maybe even inspires some to consider journalism or media careers. The American Revolution

VPM proudly hosted the country's first screening of The American Revolution in March at the Altria Theater, featuring acclaimed filmmakers Ken Burns, Sarah Botstein and David Schmidt. We were honored to welcome Ken Burns and Sarah Botstein to Virginia once again on July 3 for a special donor gathering in Charlottesville, continuing the excitement and countdown.
