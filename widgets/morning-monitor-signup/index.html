General Assembly Passes Budget with
Historic Education Funding Increase

By Sarah Mitchell · VPM News · March 10, 2026

Virginia lawmakers approved a two-year state budget late Thursday that includes a record $1.2 billion increase in K–12 education funding, ending weeks of negotiations between the House and Senate over how to distribute money from a stronger-than-expected revenue surplus.

The spending plan, which now heads to Governor's desk for signature, raises per-pupil funding by 14 percent and allocates $340 million to teacher pay increases statewide. Democrats and Republicans both claimed credit for the package, calling it a generational investment in public education.
